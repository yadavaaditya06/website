About

Aaditya Raj Yadav

I’m an undergraduate in the Department of Physics at IIT Kanpur. I work at the seam between quantum fluid dynamics and machine learning for physics — mostly asking what neural operators learn when they’re trained on turbulent data, and how to make them faster on real hardware.

aadityarajyadav@iitk.ac.in · GitHub · CV

Research interests

Neural operators & spectral learning

Adaptive Fourier Neural Operators (AFNO), FNO variants, and how their behaviour in $k$-space — entropy, energy redistribution, learned band-pass structure — reflects the physics they were trained on.

Quantum fluids

Gross-Pitaevskii dynamics in 2D and 3D: vortex lattices in rotating BECs, quantum turbulence, and the inertial-range scaling $E(k) \sim k^{-5/3}$ in the compressible kinetic spectrum.

LLM-assisted scientific computing

Using frontier models to synthesize, autotune, and verify CUDA kernels for split-step Fourier solvers on NVIDIA A100 / H100. The interesting question isn’t “can it write a kernel” — it’s how to close the correctness loop cheaply.
