Quantum Fluids, Neural Operators, and the Entropy of Learning

I study how learned spectral models — neural operators trained on physical data — represent and compress turbulent dynamics. The central object is the Gross-Pitaevskii equation,

$$ i\hbar \, \partial_t \psi = \left( -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 + V(\mathbf{r}) + g|\psi|^2 \right)\psi $$

whose vortex-lattice and turbulence regimes provide a clean testbed for asking: what does a neural operator actually learn about a cascade?

Current research

In progress · 2026

Spectral entropy across AFNO blocks in FourCastNet

We probe NVIDIA’s FourCastNet by injecting two reference inputs — isotropic white noise and Kolmogorov-distributed turbulence — and measuring per-layer spectral entropy

$$ S(\ell) = -\sum_{k} \hat{p}_k(\ell) \, \log \hat{p}_k(\ell), \quad \hat{p}_k(\ell) = \frac{|E_k(\ell)|^2}{\sum_{k'} |E_{k'}(\ell)|^2} $$

across the Adaptive Fourier Neural Operator stack. The drop in $S(\ell)$ for Kolmogorov inputs — absent for white noise — suggests the network has learned a soft band-pass that approximates an inertial-range filter.

In progress · 2026

LLM-synthesized CUDA kernels for Gross-Pitaevskii solvers

Pipeline: a frontier LLM emits candidate split-step Fourier kernels for $-\nabla^2 + g|\psi|^2$ on A100/H100, an autotuner sweeps tile sizes, and a correctness oracle checks energy and norm conservation. Best kernels reach $\sim 1.7\times$ the baseline cuFFT pipeline on $4096^2$ grids.

Course project · 2025

Vortex lattice formation in rotating BECs

Imaginary-time evolution of a 2D BEC in a rotating harmonic trap, $V(r) = \tfrac{1}{2}m\omega^2 r^2 - \Omega L_z$. Tracking the critical rotation $\Omega_c$ at which the first vortex enters the cloud as a function of $g$ recovers the expected logarithmic dependence on the healing length.
